Introduction To Bullying

Bullying is unwanted, aggressive behavior that involves a real or perceived power imbalance. It can be in various of forms which includes, physical, verbal, and emotional abuse, often repeated over time.

About 1 in 5 students (20%) reports being bullied in school, according to the National Center for Educational Statistics (2021).

The most reported type of bullying would be verbal.

The impact of bullying on victims is decreasing of mental health; victims are at higher risk of depression, anxitey, and low self-esteem. As well as academic performance; people who are bullied often experience declining grades and decreased school participation. To add on, its also can pervent suicide risk: Victims of bullying are more likely to consider or attempt suicide. Cyberbullying, in particular, is linked to an increased risk of self-harm.
